01.1 — Core Narrative

The Copy Trading Problem

One hook. One pain. Every piece of content, every trailer, every community pitch traces back to this single documented truth that every Solana trader has experienced personally.

The single hook — never deviate from this
"They know you're watching.
Now trade like they don't."
Every serious Solana trader has been copy traded. They've watched their own buy get front-run within the same block. They've seen wallets farm copy traders — buy at $10k MC, let bots pump to $50k, dump instantly on followers. Wallet tracking tools like GMGN, Birdeye, and DEXScreener make every wallet a public trading profile. CLOAK is the only product that makes you invisible to these actors. That's the entire pitch.
The four documented pain pointsREAL
1
Wallet tracking is universal
GMGN, Birdeye, DEXScreener — every buy is visible to thousands of bots and degens instantly. You are public by default. There is no opt-out on a public blockchain.
2
Copy traders farm the people they follow
Documented: whales deliberately buy dead coins at $10k MC, copy bots pump it to $50k, whale insta-exits on their own followers. If you're being copied, you are their exit liquidity.
3
Strategies exposed before execution completes
Any accumulation position, DCA schedule, or limit order is visible on-chain before complete. Your edge disappears the moment the mempool sees your intent.
4
KOL wallets are public exit machines
KOL wallets on CT are listed on tracking sites. Their followers copy them. The KOLs know this — they either front-run their community or post entries after the fact. Either way, followers lose.

02.3 — Quant Exit Model

Volume-Anchored Exit Engine

Calibrated from GhostwareOS real data. Vol/MC decay follows an exponential curve with λ = 0.38/day. Exit cap is 10% of daily volume, spread across multiple transactions each capped at 3% of pool depth. The formula maximises extraction while keeping individual sells invisible on the chart.

The formula — all terms defined
// Step 1: Project daily volume using empirical decay curve
Vol(t) = MC_peak × V₀ × e−λt
λ = 0.38/day (GhostwareOS comp) | lower λ if flywheel active (target 0.28–0.32)
V₀ = Day 1 vol/MC ratio — Ghost ~100–140% ATH; CLOAK target 75–90% (net buy+sell)

// Step 2: Maximum safe extraction per day
MaxExit(t) = Vol(t) × 0.10  // 10% of daily volume — undetected, non-damaging

// Step 3: Per-transaction hard floor — protect chart
PerTx(t) = min(PoolDepth(t) × 0.03, MaxExit(t) / TxCount)
PoolDepth(t) = MC × (3% + t×0.5%) // grows as SOL accumulates in pool

// Step 4: Price impact verification per sell
Impact(t) = PerTx / (PoolDepth + PerTx) × 100  // target < 3%

// Step 5: Optimal transaction count
OptimalTx = ceil(MaxExit / min(PoolDepth × 0.03, MaxExit / 3))
